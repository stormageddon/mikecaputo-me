Budget Calculator - Active development

Budget Calculator started when I became super into finances. I read everything I could about budgeting, investing, 401ks, roth accounts, taxes, cutting costs, making money; everything money related I could get my hands on. While reading about it, I came to the realization that I wanted a tool that went above and beyond the tools like YNAB and Mint that I was using because these only really helped with my budget if I already had a budget in mind. What I really wanted was a tool that helped me to create that budget from scratch with only 1 number - my salary.

Well, my salary plus federal taxes, state taxes, tax withholdings, investment dividends, and everything else that can actually impact the amount of money I actually have. And that's what the Budget Calculator is meant to do. Given a salary, it will extract your actual spendable money and then take proven budget breakdowns (for example, the general rule of thumb is to not spend more than 30% of your take home pay on rent or your mortgage) and apply them to give you a solid idea of what your budget should look like. From there, the calculator allows you to adjust the different categories (I want to spend 50% of my pay on rent because this apartment is BALLER!) and it will intelligently update other categories within your budget based on your preferences in the adjusted categories to give you a livable budget that is withing your monthly income.

FastChat - Live Beta

FastChat was built pretty much on a whim. During my last year of college I worked with a couple of friends to develop this chat application in our spare time because we were working on a school project that required lots of communication and the options out there just weren't cutting it. We tried texting, Google hangouts, GroupMe, and a few others but nothing really worked the way we wanted it to. Most of them had issues between iOS and Android devices or didn't have enough options (web interface + phone interface) or were just too clunky to use. So we prototyped an mvp of a chat app over a weekend and began using that. And we used that application to communicate within not only our project group, but also our group of friends for the rest of the year!

FastChat is at a stable and runnable state. We recently made the decision to open source it, so feel free to clone it from GitHub and add some features! The goal is to have an easy to use, near-instant messaging system that allows people to send text, images, video, whatever they want to a group of friends. It's been a great project to work on and there is still plenty more to do for it!
